Generic time trackers weren't built for support

Toggl and Clockify track "time on a project." Support leaders need to know time per customer, per ticket type, and whether the day was spent firefighting or on real work.

🎯

Activity types that fit support

Issue, customer call, ticket troubleshooting, follow-up, escalation/on-call, Pylon & Linear clean-up — not just "billable hours."

🏢

Time by customer

See which accounts are eating your team's hours. Spot the high-support customers before they churn — or before you under-price them.

📊

Manager rollup

One private view for leads: totals by agent, by customer, by activity — with date filters and CSV export. Agents only see their own time.

⚡

One-click timer or manual log

Start a timer that keeps running in the background, or log minutes after the fact. Pinned browser extension means zero friction.

🎚

Daily target tracking

Set your shift and break; every day shows tracked vs. target with a progress bar. Know who's overloaded and who's got capacity.

🔒

Private by design

Per-company data isolation, Google sign-in, admin-only analytics. Your team's data never mixes with anyone else's.

Who it's for

✅ A great fit if you…

  • Run a support, CS, or implementation team
  • Want to know time-per-customer and time-per-activity
  • Need to justify headcount or spot overloaded agents
  • Live in tools like Pylon, Zendesk, Linear, Slack

🚫 Not for you if…

  • You need invoicing & client billing (use Harvest)

  • You only care about dev sprint time
