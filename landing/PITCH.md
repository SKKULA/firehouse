# Firehouse — one-page pitch

**Time & effort analytics for support teams.**

## The problem
Support, CS, and implementation teams don't know where their hours actually go. Generic time
trackers (Toggl, Clockify, Harvest) are built for freelancers and billable projects — they track
"time on a project," not "time per **customer**, per **ticket type**." So support leaders can't
answer basic questions: Which accounts eat our hours? Is the team firefighting or doing real
work? Who's overloaded? Do we need another hire?

## The product
Firehouse is a lightweight tracker built around how support teams actually work:
- **Support-native activity types** — issue, customer call, ticket troubleshooting, follow-up,
  escalation/on-call, Pylon & Linear clean-up.
- **Time by customer & by activity** — see which accounts and which work consume the day.
- **Manager rollup** — one private view for leads (by agent / customer / activity, with date
  filters + CSV). Agents see only their own time.
- **Frictionless capture** — pinned browser extension with a background timer, or quick manual
  log. Daily target vs. shift hours.

## Who it's for
Support / CS / implementation team leads at SaaS companies (10–200 people) who live in Pylon,
Zendesk, Linear, Slack — and need to justify headcount, price support correctly, or spot churn-
risk accounts by support load.

## Why now / why us
Built originally for an internal CS team at an AI-native ATS — so the workflow is real, not
guessed. The wedge is **vertical focus**: we don't compete with Toggl on features, we win the
narrow "support team effort analytics" use case generic tools ignore.

## Business model
SaaS, per-seat. Free up to 3 agents; **$6/agent/mo** for teams; custom for scale (SSO,
integrations). Indicative — validating willingness-to-pay via the waitlist.

## What we're testing right now
Demand. The landing page + waitlist measures whether support leaders want this enough to raise
their hand. Target signal before building the multi-tenant SaaS: **3+ teams** who say they'd pay,
with a number.
